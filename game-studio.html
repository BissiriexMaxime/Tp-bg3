Larian Studios is an independent video game developer founded in 2004 by Larian As. The name Larian comes from the Breton word «larian» which means «grandiose». The studios started their careers with games like Baldur’s Gate II: Shadows of Amn and Baldur’s Gate II: Dagger of Honour before being bought by Atari in 2006. In 2008, they are taken over by the French group Softmicro and continue to work on projects such as The Elder Scrolls IV: Oblivion and Fallout 3.

Larian Studios dominated the 2023 Game Awards, with Baldur’s Gate 3 winning six awards, including Game of the Year. In addition to the game of the year, Baldur’s Gate 3 received the Best RPG, Best Multiplayer, Best Community Support and Player Choice awards, while actor Neil Newbon won the Best Performance for his role as Astarion.
